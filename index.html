What talks' ratings tell us

Introduction

TED talks are influential videos from expert speakers on technology, entertainment, design, science, business and a variety of topics, with subtitles in over 100 languages. On TED's official website, audience can review and see ratings for each talk. In this information visualization project, we have analyzed a dataset of 2550 TED talks and gotten some insights on how does the rating system work and the relation between a talk's views and ratings.

I. Distribution of People's Opinions

As a quick way to express opinions on a talk, the TED website provides audience with 14 voting options covering positive, negative, and neutral ratings.
Positive: Beautiful, Courageous, Fascinating, Funny, Informative, Ingenious, Inspiring, Persuasive
Negative: Confusing, Longwinded, Obnoxious, Unconvincing
Neutral: OK, Jaw-dropping

By looking into the total of votes for each rating, we found that Inspiring, Informative, and Fascinating have been frequently used in general, while the negative ones have been less present. Then, we identified the "Main Rating" of each talk (on which a talk has received the highest votes) and visualized the distribution of these main ratings. It reveals the same pattern as total votes.
